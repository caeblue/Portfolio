❖ Our Goal ❖

To learn the next steps of sustainable energy and healthy eating by studying the peatland's carbon intake. Along with studies in self-sustainability, new farming practices, and the technology that allows Scotland to use majority green energy.

Environmental Research Institute

The Environmental Research Institute (ERI) is part of a larger collection of research institutes within the North Highland College in Scotland. The main themes of research within the institute revolve around climate change, energy, and health. While I was visiting, we measured carbon samples from the natural peatlands and looked into the importance of sphagnum moss within the bogs. Along with attending conferences about drones, solar energy, wind turbines, and seaweed based fish farms for food resources.

Findhorn Eco-Village

Findhorn is one of The largest Intentional communities in Britain founded in 1962. An intentional community is a group of people designed to have a large amount of social cohesion and teamwork. While I was visiting, we met the people living at the community and shared practices while touring the town. The town's goal is to leave as little of a carbon foot print as possible, making it an eco-village. Almost all meals are made from things grown in the village and different forms of filtration by plants to take care of their waste.
